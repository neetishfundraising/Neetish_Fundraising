His Story

Neetish Choudkoury, aged 38, from Camp Rabot, Rivière du Poste, Mauritius, is battling a rare and debilitating neurological disease called Refractory Generalised Dystonia — causing severe, uncontrollable muscle contractions that affect his entire body and his ability to live a normal life.

The only hope is Deep Brain Stimulation (DBS) surgery — a US-FDA approved procedure where electrodes are implanted in key brain regions to regulate abnormal brain activity, dramatically reducing muscle spasms and restoring motor control. This surgery must be performed in India.

His family has been officially authorised by the Commissioner's Office of Mauritius under the Public Collections Act to raise funds for his treatment.
